Skill Focus
Jesse's Opening Meditation
Genuine stillness and full breath. Watch for the group actually settling — not just going quiet. Agreements should land, not just be recited.
Zip Zap Zop
Eye contact before the throw, not after. Clean receipt before passing on. Speed without sloppiness. In variants, watch for clean transitions rather than confusion masking as energy.
Bunny Bunny
Full physical commitment to the bit — no half-gestures. Rhythm staying intact under pressure. Recovery from errors without self-judgment or apologizing to the circle.
Mind Meld
Speed of association without overthinking. Willingness to converge toward a partner. No editing the first instinct — the unfiltered word is the point.
Four Square
Consistent relationships within each scene across rotations. Clean context switches — no bleed between squares. Scene memory staying accurate, not drifting over time.
4 of 6
Skill Focus (cont.)
Freeze Tag
Strong, specific body positions worth tagging. Bold justifications that commit to the new scene. Scenes that build enough before being tagged — not constant interruption.
Foreign Film
Emotional specificity in gibberish — accent and feeling, not just noise. Translator supporting the scene, not correcting or competing with it. Scene logic staying intact across the language gap.
Story, Story, Die!
Immediate pickup with no dead air. Narrative thread staying coherent across handoffs. Graceful elimination — dying is part of the fun, not a failure moment.
Montage
Clean edits that don't step on scenes. Thematic callbacks emerging naturally. Ensemble awareness — no one player carrying alone. Transitions building energy rather than breaking it.
Five Things
Whole group counting together in unison. No hesitation penalty — speed over perfection. Genuine celebration of every answer, fast or slow. High energy exit that sends people out well.
